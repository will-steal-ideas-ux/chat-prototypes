Key Findings

The cybersecurity landscape has undergone significant transformation in 2025, driven by the proliferation of AI-powered attack vectors and the increasing sophistication of nation-state threat actors. Our analysis of 2,400 incidents across 47 countries reveals three critical patterns that will define the 2026 security posture for enterprise organizations.

  • AI-augmented phishing attacks increased by 340% year-over-year, with detection rates dropping to 23% for traditional email security platforms
  • Supply chain compromises now account for 41% of all critical infrastructure breaches, up from 28% in 2024
  • Mean time to detect (MTTD) improved to 16 days industry-wide, though critical sectors lag at 31 days

Market Overview

Global cybersecurity spending reached $284B in 2025, representing a 14.3% increase year-over-year. The acceleration is attributed to regulatory mandates, high-profile breach disclosures, and the rapid adoption of cloud-native infrastructure demanding new security architectures.
